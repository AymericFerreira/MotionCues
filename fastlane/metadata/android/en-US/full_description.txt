Motion Cues helps reduce the car sickness many people feel when reading or using a phone as a passenger.

When your eyes are fixed on a screen but your inner ear feels the vehicle move, the mismatch can cause nausea. Motion Cues draws a field of dots over whatever you're using that drift in response to the vehicle's motion — sliding as you turn, accelerate, and brake — giving your peripheral vision a cue that matches what your body feels. It's inspired by Apple's Vehicle Motion Cues, built for Android.

HOW IT WORKS
• The dots are driven by your phone's accelerometer — no GPS, no internet.
• Turn left and the dots slide right; brake and they move up. The effect is subtle and sits behind your content.
• Works over everyday apps like e-readers, browsers, and articles.

CUSTOMIZE EVERYTHING (free)
• Dot color, number, and size
• Motion sensitivity
• Background dimming
• Dynamic drifting dots, or a static reference grid

AUTOMATIC OR MANUAL
• Auto-start when it senses you're in a moving vehicle (motion sensors only — no location)
• Or toggle it yourself from the app or a Quick Settings tile

PRIVATE BY DESIGN
• No data collection, no analytics, no ads, no network access
• Everything runs on your device; your settings never leave your phone
• Free and open-source (GPLv3)

GOOD TO KNOW
The dots appear over your apps, but Android does not yet let any third-party app draw over the system status bar, notification shade, Quick Settings, or lock screen. Every app of this kind shares that limit.
